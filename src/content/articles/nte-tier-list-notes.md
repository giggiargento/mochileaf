Tier labels on Mochileaf are a **compass, not a verdict**. We group characters by how welcoming they feel in everyday play—not by day-one banner hype or spreadsheet panic.

## What we look at

- **Story and presentation** — Does the character feel complete in cutscenes and exploration?
- **Accessibility** — Can a newer player enjoy them without perfect gear?
- **Daily comfort** — Are they pleasant to bring along for commissions and casual anomalies?

We also note when someone shines in City Tycoon, co-op, or later chapters—but we avoid calling anyone “mandatory.”

## What we skip

Patch-week rankings copied from speedrun communities, whale-only comparisons, and language like “must pull” or “dead unit.” Those change too fast and rarely match a cozy playstyle.

## Using the list

Treat tiers as invitations to explore, not orders to rebuild your roster. If you love an A-tier face more than an S-tier banner star, keep them on the team—the point of NTE on Mochileaf is finding characters you enjoy spending time with.
