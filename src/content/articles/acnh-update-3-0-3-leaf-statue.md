Animal Crossing: New Horizons is having a surprisingly busy 2026. After January's large **version 3.0** return—resort hotel, Slumber Islands, bigger storage, and quality-of-life crafting—the game received a smaller but meaningful patch on **April 29**: **version 3.0.3**.

If you stepped away after the January excitement, this one is worth a gentle check-in.

## What 3.0.3 changes

Nintendo's patch notes highlight one player-facing addition: the **25th-anniversary leaf statue** is now available through **Nook Shopping's furniture catalog**, not only as a one-time gift tied to updating.

That matters for decorators who missed the earlier distribution window—you can plan the statue into a museum garden or anniversary corner without worrying you locked yourself out forever. Nintendo also notes a **price adjustment** for the item alongside the catalog change.

The rest of the update is maintenance: minor bug fixes aimed at stability on both Nintendo Switch and Switch 2 versions of the game.

## Context: a big January, then anniversary touches

Version **3.0** arrived in mid-January 2026 alongside the **Nintendo Switch 2 Edition**—the first major content drop in years, with resort hospitality, dream-style island decorating, expanded home storage (up to thousands of items, including plants), bulk crafting, and more.

Version **3.0.2** in April added the commemorative leaf statue as a gift for players who updated, part of wider **Animal Crossing series 25th-anniversary** celebrations (including music on Nintendo Music).

3.0.3 simply makes that celebratory piece easier to obtain for everyone going forward.

## A cozy way to revisit your island

You do not need a checklist for this patch. Log in, open Nook Shopping, see if the leaf statue fits a spot you have been saving, and wander the island for ten minutes.

If January's 3.0 update still sits uninstalled, consider downloading both—you get the resort and Slumber Islands *and* the anniversary collectible path in one calm evening.
