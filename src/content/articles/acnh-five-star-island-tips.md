Five stars are a **compliment from Isabelle**, not a report card. You are aiming for an island that feels lived-in and inviting—not a sterile theme park built in one weekend.

## Clutter and charm

Too many loose items lying around can lower your score, but cozy corners still count as design. Store extras in sheds, use the recycle box, and leave breathing room near Resident Services.

## Fencing and paths

Defined edges help visitors read your island. You do not need perfect grids—gentle curves and short fence runs already make spaces feel intentional.

## Flowers and trees

Balance blooms with open grass so the island does not read as overwhelming. If hybrids stress you out, standard flowers in favorite colors are more than enough.

## Take your time

Check ratings when you are proud of a area, not every morning. Stars can tick up quietly after small tidy sessions rather than one huge terraform push.
